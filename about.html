About Tim Hortons

Our History

Tim Hortons is a Canadian chain of cafeterias and fast food restaurants that was founded in 1964 by former hockey player Tim Horton and his partner, Jim Charade. Tim Hortons' history is marked by exceptional growth and has become an iconic part of Canadian culture.

The first Tim Hortons store opened in Hamilton, Ontario, Canada, and quickly gained popularity for its high-quality coffee and delicious baked goods, especially its donuts. The company continued to expand, and in 1967, it became a franchise, further fueling its expansion across Canada.

In the years that followed, Tim Hortons experienced massive success and became one of the largest and most beloved restaurant chains in Canada. The company diversified its menu to include a variety of sandwiches, wraps, salads, and other breakfast and lunch items, in addition to its famous coffee and donuts.

Tim Hortons continued to grow in Canada and in other parts of the world. The brand has become a symbol of national identity for Canadians and has expanded its global presence with stores in several countries.

Throughout its history, Tim Hortons has been involved in various philanthropic initiatives and has supported local communities in Canada through programs such as "Tim Hortons Day Camp" for underprivileged children.

Our Objectives

Our goal is to provide customers with an exceptional and satisfying experience every time they visit one of your stores. In addition, Tim Hortons strives to establish itself as a cultural icon in Canada and expand its global presence, taking its brand and products to different parts of the world. Its focus on quality, freshness and excellence in customer service has allowed it to become one of the most beloved and recognized restaurant chains in Canada and beyond.
